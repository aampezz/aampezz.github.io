Zydeco iPad App

UI/UX Strategy and Design

Zydeco is a mobile application for middle-school students and teachers that connects Common Core scientific inquiry curriculum across in-school and out-of-school contexts.


Since early 2012, I have collaborated with developers and curriculum experts to design desktop, tablet, and smartphone applications for Zydeco, an educational technology (pseudo) start-up funded by the National Science Foundation.


Zydeco launched in the Apple App Store in 2013 and v2 is anticipated in May 2014.


How It Works

Students and teachers set up an investigation in the classroom prior to any field work in the out-of-class setting (e.g. museum), creating an open-ended driving question or prompt for their inquiry.


Then, students capture data (photo, video, audio, or text) and information on-site at the museum they are exploring. They use their devices to add labels, text notes, and/or audio notes to annotate the data. Students sync their devices to the cloud so that all data can be accessed from any device.


Back at school, students review and synthesize both their own and peer-collected information, all to develop a scientific explanation, using the claim-evidence-reasoning framework, that addresses their driving question. All of this is done on the mobile app, including the ability to present to their classmates.
